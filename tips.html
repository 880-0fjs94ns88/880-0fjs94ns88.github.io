Keep Your Location Private

Websites can figure out where you are using your IP, browser, or Wi-Fi. Here’s how to stop that from happening:

Use a VPN

Mask your IP and browse from another country. Stay hidden online.

Good options: NordVPN, ExpressVPN, ProtonVPN

Turn Off Browser Location

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy & security → Site Settings → Location → Block
  • Firefox: about:config → geo.enabled → false
  • Safari: System Settings → Privacy → Location Services → Off for Safari

Use Privacy-Focused Browsers

  • Brave: Blocks ads and trackers
  • Tor: Bounces traffic through multiple nodes

Add Privacy Extensions

  • uBlock Origin: Block ads and trackers
  • NoScript: Stop scripts unless you approve them
  • CanvasBlocker: Prevent fingerprinting

Use a Private DNS

Don’t let your DNS requests reveal your location. Try these:

  • Cloudflare: 1.1.1.1
  • Google DNS: 8.8.8.8
  • NextDNS

Avoid Public Wi-Fi

  • Use mobile data or a VPN when you can
  • Don’t sign into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi

TL;DR

Use a VPN, block browser location, install privacy tools, and avoid sketchy networks.
